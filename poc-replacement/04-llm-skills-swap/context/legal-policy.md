# Acme Legal & Compliance Policy for Editorial Content

## Scope
Applies to all published articles, newsletters, and sponsored content.

## Hard prohibitions (do not publish)
1. **No medical promises.** Never claim that a food, supplement, or behavior "cures",
   "prevents", or "treats" any disease. Replace with "may support", "is associated with",
   or cite the specific study finding.
2. **No securities advice.** Never suggest a specific asset, stock, or investment is a
   "buy", "guaranteed return", or "safe bet". Financial content must include the
   disclaimer "This is not financial advice."
3. **No defamation.** Never accuse a named person or company of a crime, fraud, or
   misconduct without documented evidence and legal review.
4. **No GDPR-triggering data collection language.** Do not imply the reader is being
   tracked or profiled without our privacy policy being linked.
5. **No competitor disparagement by name.** Critiques of competitor products must refer
   to "some products in this category" rather than naming the brand, unless a
   verifiable public fact is cited.

## Required disclaimers
- Health articles: "This content is for informational purposes only and is not medical
  advice. Consult a qualified healthcare provider."
- Financial articles: "This is not financial advice. Past performance does not predict
  future returns."
- Legal/tax articles: "This is not legal or tax advice."

## Attribution
- Every clinical statistic must cite its primary source (author, year, journal) OR a
  link to the official source.
- Government data (BLS, Fed, CDC) must link to the specific page, not the agency homepage.
- Unsourced claims must be removed or clearly labeled as the author's opinion.

## Tone and framing
- Never use absolute terms ("always", "never", "100%", "guaranteed") for outcomes.
- Avoid urgency-inducing language on financial content ("act now", "last chance").
- Do not personalize or target individual reader behaviors without consent language.
