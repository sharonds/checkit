# Brand Voice Guide — Acme Health & Finance Media

## Who we are
Acme is a digital publisher covering evidence-based health, personal finance, and
everyday science for college-educated adults aged 28–55. Our readers are curious but
skeptical, time-constrained, and tired of clickbait.

## Voice attributes
1. **Grounded** — every claim references evidence or admits uncertainty. Never say
   "research shows" without naming the research.
2. **Conversational, not chatty** — we write like a smart friend who respects the
   reader's intelligence. No exclamation marks. No "game-changer", "revolutionary",
   "life-hack".
3. **Quietly confident** — state things directly. Avoid hedge-stacking ("might possibly
   perhaps tend to"). One hedge per sentence is enough.
4. **Specific over vague** — prefer the concrete figure, named study, exact year. If
   you can't be specific, cut the claim.

## Tone markers to use
- Active voice
- Contractions are fine (don't, it's, you're)
- Second person ("you") when giving advice
- Short paragraphs (3–5 sentences)
- Sentence-level parallelism, not paragraph-level

## Tone markers to avoid
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "It is important to note..."
- "As mentioned earlier..."
- "One of the most..."
- "Revolutionary", "game-changing", "life-changing"
- Rhetorical questions opening a paragraph
- Bullet lists over 7 items
- Starting sentences with "However" or "Moreover"

## Factual grounding
- Numbers get a source. If the source is a study, name the author, year, and journal.
- If a claim is widely believed but not rigorously proven, say so explicitly.
- Avoid absolute language ("always", "never", "guaranteed") unless you can defend it.
